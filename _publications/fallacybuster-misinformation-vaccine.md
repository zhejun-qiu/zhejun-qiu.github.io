title: "Correcting Misinformation in an Authoritarian Context: Logical Fallacy Training via Short Videos in China"
coauthors: "Yuyang Pu and Muyao Hang"
status: "R & R at Political Psychology"
abstract: 'Logical fallacies are often embedded in misinformation to mislead the public and manipulate opinion. How can we help individuals resist such tactics? Existing research offers a variety of interventions to counter misinformation, yet the asymmetry between misinformation production and correction, the underrepresentation of authoritarian and non-English speaking contexts, and motivated reasoning continue to limit their efficacy and external validity. We introduce a refined, "vaccine-like" intervention: a series of AI-generated short videos that train individuals to recognize and resist three logical fallacies commonly found in misinformation—false causality, hasty generalization, and personal attack. We conducted a preregistered survey experiment (N = 2,416) in China in August 2025 to evaluate the efficacy of the intervention. Results show that participants exposed to the logical fallacy training were less likely to endorse related misinformation overall, with particularly strong effects among more vulnerable groups, including nationalist participants and those with lower cognitive reflection ability. Our study contributes to the literature on misinformation correction by extending external validity to an understudied authoritarian context and by designing an intervention that bypasses potential censorship through non-political, reasoning-based, fable-like narratives. In addition, using generative AI in combination with short-form videos substantially lowers the costs of replicating, implementing, and receiving the intervention for all parties involved in misinformation correction. Finally, our intervention strengthens cognitive immunity among those most susceptible to misinformation.'
