---
title: "Do Refugees Fuel Conflict, or Just Make It Visible?"
coauthors: "Christopher W. Blair and Burcu Savun"
status: "Submitted"
abstract: 'Refugee inflows are often linked to increases in reported violence in host communities. We argue that this association may be partly attributable to changes in observability. Refugee inflows draw humanitarian actors, security forces, and media attention into peripheral areas, increasing the likelihood that incidents are detected even when underlying conflict dynamics shift little. This reporting-induced bias should be more pronounced for low-scale non-state violence than state-based violence and in areas with large refugee settlements. We evaluate these claims by combining global subnational analyses with micro-level evidence from Afghanistan and using novel reporting-quality proxies. Areas hosting refugees appear to experience more communal violence and protests, but much of this pattern coincides with increased media presence and higher reporting quality. While we cannot fully recover true violence levels or adjust for this bias, our findings underscore how ignoring detection dynamics can yield misleading inferences about the effects of refugee hosting on violence.'
